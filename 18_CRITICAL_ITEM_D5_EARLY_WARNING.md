## WHY D5 IS CRITICAL

**D5 adalah evidence bahwa institusi care tentang student success—bukan hanya enrollment numbers.**

Accreditation scoring (BAN-PT):
- ✓ Institutions dengan early warning system = better retention = better educational quality score
- ✗ Institutions tanpa system = higher drop-out = lower quality score

Real impact:
- Students identified early → intervention → can graduate on time
- Without early intervention → students struggle silently → eventually drop-out → institutional failure rate naik

## D5 DATA SOURCES: MANA DAPAT DATA RISK?

### Source 1: Academic Performance (dari WK1)
```
Students at risk:
□ IPK < 2.0 (academic failure threshold)
□ Nilai < C untuk critical courses (signal struggling)
□ Course repetition (took course 2x, still didn't pass)
□ Failed prerequisite (can't progress to next level)
```

**Ask WK1:** "Siapa saja mahasiswa dengan nilai di bawah threshold?"

### Source 2: Financial (dari Admin/Finance)
```
Students at risk:
□ Belum bayar tuition (3+ bulan)
□ Cicilan tersendat-sendat
□ Request bantuan financial (inconsistent payment history)
□ Keluarga report financial hardship
```

**Ask Admin:** "Siapa saja dengan payment issues?"

### Source 3: Attendance (dari Dosen / Akademik System)
```
Students at risk:
□ Absensi tinggi (>20% absent)
□ Pattern: Absent Jumat/Senin (suspect skipping)
□ Stop attending after mid-semester (sign of withdrawal)
```

**Ask Dosen pembimbing:** "Siapa yang sering absent?"

### Source 4: Personal/Behavioral (dari WK3, BEM, counselor)
```
Students at risk:
□ Personal crisis reported (keluarga masalah, kesehatan)
□ Mental health issues (anxiety, depression signals)
□ Relationship problems affecting studies
□ Drug/alcohol concerns
□ Bullying atau peer conflict
```

**Gather from:** Konselor, dosen pembina, BEM leadership, student complaints

### Source 5: Status Changes (dari Admin SIAKAD)
```
Students at risk:
□ Requested cuti (sign of struggle)
□ Previously cuti, now coming back (needs extra support)
□ Tidak registrasi semester terakhir (silent withdrawal)
```

## D5 RISK SCORING & SEGMENTATION

### Scoring Model:

**HIGH RISK (Intervention URGENT):**
- IPK < 1.5 AND (Attend < 60% OR Absent payment)
- Score: 3+ risk factors present
- **Action:** Assign dedicated mentor/konselor, weekly check-in

**MEDIUM RISK (Monitor closely):**
- IPK 1.5-2.0 OR Payment issue OR High absensi
- Score: 2 risk factors present
- **Action:** Mentor outreach, academic support offer, bi-weekly check-in

**LOW RISK (Standard support):**
- IPK 2.0-2.5 (borderline) OR 1 minor risk factor
- Score: 1 risk factor
- **Action:** Regular program support (konseling available if asked)

### Risk Factor Matrix:

| Factor | HIGH | MEDIUM | LOW |
|--------|------|--------|-----|
| Academic (IPK) | <1.5 | 1.5-2.0 | 2.0-2.5 |
| Financial | Unpaid 3+ mo | Cicilan irregular | Minimal issue |
| Attendance | <60% | 60-80% | >80% |
| Personal | Crisis reported | Indicator | None |
| **TOTAL RISK** | 3+ factors | 2 factors | 1 factor |

## D5 EXECUTION STEPS

### STEP 1: DATA GATHERING (Early July)

**Timeline:** 2-3 hari

1. **Email WK1:** "Beritahu siapa saja dengan IPK <2.0 atau nilai failure."
2. **Email Admin:** "Siapa belum bayar tuition? Siapa cuti?"
3. **Contact Dosen pembina:** "Siapa sering absent? Ada concern dari classroom?"
4. **Review konselor notes:** Siapa yang sudah report masalah pribadi?
5. **Check BEM/student feedback:** Ada report bullying, conflict, health issues?

**Compile into:** Spreadsheet dengan semua nama + risk factors

### STEP 2: RISK SCORING (1-2 hari)

Using matrix above:
- Assign score untuk setiap mahasiswa
- Categorize: HIGH / MEDIUM / LOW

```
Example:
----
Mahasiswa A: IPK 1.2 + Unpaid 2 mo + 50% attend = HIGH RISK ➜ Score 3
Mahasiswa B: IPK 1.8 + Normal attend + No payment issue = MEDIUM RISK ➜ Score 1
Mahasiswa C: IPK 2.3 + Normal attend + No issue = LOW RISK ➜ Score 0
```

### STEP 3: INTERVENTION PLANNING (Per mahasiswa HIGH RISK)

For each HIGH RISK student:

**☑ Assign Mentor/Konselor:**
- Who: Dosen pembina atau WK3 staff
- Tugasnya: Weekly check-in, support academic recovery, refer ke layanan

**☑ Tailor Intervention:**
- Academic: Tutoring? Remedial course? Study group?
- Financial: Payment plan? Beasiswa bantuan? Part-time work?
- Personal: Konseling? Mentoring? Referral ke health services?
- Family: Involve parents (coordinate meeting)?

**☑ Document Plan:**
```
Student Name: [X]
Risk Category: HIGH
Risk Factors: [List]
Assigned Mentor: [Name & Contact]
Intervention: [Specific, actionable steps]
Timeline: [When will intervene start?]
Success Metrics: [How will we measure improvement?]
```

### STEP 4: COMMUNICATE & GET BUY-IN

**To WK1:** "Ini list students at risk, koordinasi untuk academic intervention"
**To Mentors:** "Ini tugas anda—weekly check-in, support, escalate jika ada crisis"
**To HIGH RISK students:** (Send personalized letter)

**Sample letter:**
```
Assalamu'alaikum [Nama],

Kami notice anda struggling academically/financially/personally.

Kami CARE & want to help. Kami assign [Mentor name] untuk support anda.

[Mentor] will reach out minggu ini. Jangan ragu untuk share concerns—kami here to help, tidak untuk menghukum.

Bersama-sama kita bisa overcome challenges ini.

[Contact info if confidential chat needed]

WK3
```

### STEP 5: TRACKING & FOLLOW-UP

**Weekly (at least):**
- Mentor check-in dengan student
- Document conversation (notes on progress)
- Escalate jika ada crisis (health issue, financial emergency, severe emotional distress)

**Bi-weekly (WK3 review):**
- Review mentors' progress reports
- Are interventions working? Is student improving?
- Need to adjust strategy?

**Monthly (Formal review):**
- D5 review meeting (WK3, mentors, WK1 representative)
- Success rate: How many students improved?
- New risks identified? Update list

## D5 RED FLAGS TO AVOID

🚫 **Data incomplete:** Only academic data, missing financial/personal info  
→ FIX: Triangulate data from multiple sources (academic, finance, behavioral)

🚫 **High-risk list never acted upon:** List created but mentors not engaged  
→ FIX: Assign & brief mentors, confirm they understand role

🚫 **Student feels "marked"** (early warning becomes stigma)  
→ FIX: Frame as "support system," not "you're failing"

🚫 **No follow-up:** Intervention planned but never executed  
→ FIX: Weekly mentor check-in, monthly WK3 review, accountability

🚫 **One-size-fits-all:** Same intervention for all students  
→ FIX: Tailor per student need (academic vs financial vs personal)
