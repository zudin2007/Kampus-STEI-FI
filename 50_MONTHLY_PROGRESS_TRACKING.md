## BAGIAN 5: ISSUE ESCALATION PROCEDURES

### 5.1 ESCALATION PROTOCOL

**Level 1: QA Lead Response (Issues resolved within 48 hours)**

Examples:
- Missing document from one unit
- Single low-quality submission
- Scheduling conflict for one person
- Minor formatting issue

**Action:**
1. Identify issue
2. Contact responsible unit immediately
3. Provide specific feedback & deadline
4. Follow up to confirm resolution

**No escalation to leadership needed**

---

**Level 2: WK1 Involvement (Issues resolved within 1 week)**

Examples:
- Multiple units behind on deliverables
- Quality across a domain is <3/5
- Resource constraint affecting timeline
- Conflict between units

**Action:**
1. Document issue with evidence
2. Notify WK1 same day
3. Propose solution
4. WK1 authorizes action & follows up with involved parties

**Escalation:** WK1 informed; decision-making at WK1 level

---

**Level 3: Dekan Decision (Critical issues; requires institutional action)**

Examples:
- Cannot meet major deadline (collection, SAR, submission)
- Quality insufficient to submit SAR
- Major institutional resistance or conflict
- Budget/resource gap requiring Dekan allocation

**Action:**
1. Document issue comprehensively
2. Prepare briefing for Dekan
3. Present to Dekan within 24 hours
4. Get decision & direction
5. Implement Dekan's decision

**Escalation:** Dekan involved; may require institutional changes

---

**Level 4: Rektor Involvement (Program-threatening issues)**

Examples:
- Must extend timeline beyond feasibility
- Major accreditation standard may not be met
- Institutional resistance to accreditation process
- External factor (legal, regulatory) impacting accreditation

**Action:**
1. Emergency meeting with Dekan
2. Joint briefing to Rektor
3. Present options & recommendations
4. Follow Rektor's direction

**Escalation:** Rektor informed; may require strategic institutional response

## BAGIAN 6: CONTINGENCY PLANNING

### 6.1 RISK & MITIGATION MATRIX

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation | Contingency |
|------|-------------|--------|-----------|-------------|
| Unit misses collection deadline | Medium | High | Weekly reminders, clear responsibility | Extended deadline (max +2 weeks) |
| Quality of evidence is poor | Low-Medium | High | Quality rubric, training, feedback | Intensive revision cycle |
| Key personnel leave | Low | High | Cross-train staff, document processes | Backup personnel identified |
| SAR writing falls behind | Medium | High | Weekly meetings, template support | Extend review timeline |
| Accreditation body delays | Low | Medium | Early communication, flexibility | Adjust site visit prep timeline |
| Institutional crisis/emergency | Very Low | Critical | Continue accreditation work where possible | Freeze accreditation temporarily if needed |

---

### 6.2 BACKUP PLANS

**If Collection Deadline At Risk:**
- Extend collection window by 2 weeks (max to Dec 15)
- Prioritize most critical evidence
- Accept some late submissions with explanation

**If SAR Quality Insufficient:**
- Extend internal review timeline
- Bring in external editor
- Request extension from accreditation body

**If Key Personnel Unavailable:**
- Cross-train backups identified now
- Document all processes
- Keep deputy coordinators briefed
