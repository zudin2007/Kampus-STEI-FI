### 1.1 ACCREDITATION TIMELINE & SITE VISIT WINDOW

**Overall Accreditation Timeline:**

```
TA 2025/2026 & 2026/2027:
├─ Aug-Nov 2026: Evidence collection phase
├─ Dec 2026: Gap analysis & closure planning
├─ Jan 2027: SAR finalization & leadership approval
├─ Feb 1, 2027: SAR submission to accreditation body
├─ Feb-Mar 2027: Accreditation body reviews SAR
├─ Apr-May 2027: Site visit preliminary planning
├─ May-Jun 2027: SITE VISIT (Expected window)
│  └─ Duration: Typically 2-4 days
├─ Jun-Aug 2027: Accreditation body finalizes report
└─ Sep 2027: Accreditation decision & report
```

**Site Visit Window:** May 2027 - June 2027 (Anticipated)

**Rationale for May-June Timing:**
- Accreditation body needs 2-3 months after SAR submission to review
- Late April/early May: Spring semester ending (good timing for student interviews)
- Faculty less likely to be traveling (summer hasn't started)
- End of academic year allows showcase of full year's work

## BAGIAN 5: INTERVIEW PREPARATION

### 5.1 LIKELY ACCREDITOR QUESTIONS

#### About Program Mission & Outcomes

**Question 1:** "How does your program mission align with the institution's mission?"
- **Good Answer:** "Our program mission is [specific], which directly supports the institution's commitment to [institutional priority]. For example, [specific evidence of alignment]."

**Question 2:** "Can you describe your program learning outcomes and how you know students are achieving them?"
- **Good Answer:** Describe 4-6 PLOs, give assessment data showing achievement (e.g., "85% of students achieve proficiency on PLO 1, as shown in [specific assessment]").

**Question 3:** "How do you use learning outcome assessment data to improve the program?"
- **Good Answer:** Give specific example of improvement made based on data (e.g., "In 2025, we identified lower proficiency in [PLO], so we redesigned [course] to include [change], and 2026 assessment showed improvement to [%]").

---

#### About Curriculum & Teaching

**Question 4:** "How does your curriculum prepare students for their careers/further study?"
- **Good Answer:** "Our curriculum is designed around [key learning areas]. Students take [core courses] for foundations, then [advanced courses] for depth. Capstone/thesis requires [specific project]. Employer feedback shows [positive feedback]. Alumni outcomes show [X]% employed in field within 6 months."

**Question 5:** "Tell me about your teaching methods. What innovations are you using?"
- **Good Answer:** Describe 2-3 teaching innovations with evidence of student engagement/learning. Example: "We redesigned [course] to include project-based learning with real-world clients. Student engagement increased from [%] to [%], and assessment scores improved to [%]."

**Question 6:** "How do you know your students are learning?"
- **Good Answer:** "We use multiple assessment methods: [exam/project/portfolio/survey]. We assess against [PLO]. Results show [specific data]. When results fall short, we [specific action taken]."

---

#### About Faculty

**Question 7:** "Tell me about your faculty. What are their qualifications and how do you ensure teaching quality?"
- **Good Answer:** "Our [X] faculty include [X]% with PhDs and [X]% with Master's degrees. We evaluate teaching through student evaluations (average [4.2/5.0]), peer observations, and [other method]. Faculty participate in professional development ([X hours/year]), with [%] attending conferences or pursuing advanced training."

**Question 8:** "What is the research activity of your faculty?"
- **Good Answer:** "Our faculty average [X] publications per year, with [X]% in peer-reviewed journals. We have attracted [Rp X] in research funding in the past 3 years. Faculty mentor [X] undergraduate researchers annually. Research topics align with program content and ensure current, relevant curriculum."

**Question 9:** "How do you support faculty teaching development?"
- **Good Answer:** "We provide [specific supports]: professional development funds, instructional design support, teaching workshops, mentorship from senior faculty. In the past year, [X] faculty completed [specific development], resulting in [specific improvements]."

---

#### About Students & Student Success

**Question 10:** "Tell me about your students. Who do they represent, and how diverse is your student body?"
- **Good Answer:** "Our students include [describe demographics: gender, geography, first-generation, international, etc.]. Enrollment has [grown/remained stable/declined] over 3 years from [X] to [X]. We actively recruit [underrepresented groups], and [X]% of our student body represents [diversity metrics]."

**Question 11:** "What are your graduation rates and what factors influence time-to-degree?"
- **Good Answer:** "Our on-time graduation rate is [X]%, with [X]% graduating within 5 semesters. Students not on-time typically choose [reasons: double major, lighter course load, work]. We support accelerated completion through [flexible options]. Average time-to-degree is [X] semesters."

**Question 12:** "How do you support student success, especially students who struggle academically?"
- **Good Answer:** "We have early warning systems to identify at-risk students by mid-semester. Interventions include tutoring, writing support, reduced course load options, and academic advising. Success rate of interventions is [X]%, with [X]% of struggling students improving to good standing."

**Question 13:** "What happens to your graduates? Are they employed and satisfied?"
- **Good Answer:** "Our employment rate is [X]% within 6 months, with [X]% employed in-field. Graduate salaries range [Rp X - Rp Y]. Alumni satisfaction survey shows [X/5.0] rating, with [X]% reporting the program was valuable preparation. Employer satisfaction with our graduates is [X/5.0]."

---

#### About Resources & Facilities

**Question 14:** "Describe your learning resources and facilities. Are they adequate for your program?"
- **Good Answer:** "Our program has access to [specific facilities: classrooms, labs, library]. Lab equipment includes [list main equipment with ages]. All equipment is maintained on [schedule] and certified [dates]. Maintenance budget is [Rp X] annually. Recent improvements include [specific renovations]. Faculty and students report facilities are [adequate/excellent] for program delivery."

**Question 15:** "How do you ensure technology is current and supports teaching/learning?"
- **Good Answer:** "We have [X] computer labs with [current hardware]. Software includes [list key tools]. Network capacity is [describe]. IT support provides [X hours] support weekly. Faculty receive technology training [frequency]. We invest [Rp X] annually in technology to ensure currency."

**Question 16:** "Tell me about your library resources. What access do students have to information resources?"
- **Good Answer:** "Our library provides [X] books in our discipline, [X] journal subscriptions, and [X] electronic databases. Students have 24-hour access and can borrow [X books]. Librarians provide subject-specific research support. Usage data shows [X]% of students use library resources [frequency]."

---

#### About Continuous Improvement

**Question 17:** "How do you assess program effectiveness and make improvements?"
- **Good Answer:** "We conduct systematic assessment of learning outcomes annually, measuring [PLOs]. Assessment results are reviewed by faculty each [semester/year]. When results fall short of targets, we implement changes and re-assess. Examples of improvements: [2-3 specific examples with evidence]."

**Question 18:** "What are your program's strengths and where do you see areas for improvement?"
- **Good Answer:** 
  - **Strengths:** [2-3 specific areas with supporting evidence]
  - **Improvement areas:** [2-3 realistic areas with improvement plans and timelines]
  - Shows honest self-assessment and commitment to improvement

**Question 19:** "What are your strategic priorities for the next 3-5 years?"
- **Good Answer:** "We plan to [specific initiatives]: [priority 1], [priority 2], [priority 3]. These align with our mission and institutional strategy. We will measure success through [specific metrics]. Anticipated challenges and mitigation strategies include [discussion]."

---

#### Difficult Questions (How to Handle)

**Question:** "Why do you have [weakness/low metric]?"

**How to Handle:**
- Don't get defensive
- Acknowledge the issue honestly
- Explain context if relevant
- Show what you're doing about it
- Provide evidence of improvement (if applicable)

**Example Answer:**
"You're right that our [metric] was lower than target in 2024. We analyzed the issue and identified [root cause]. In response, we [specific action]. Results from 2025 show improvement to [new metric], and we anticipate reaching our target by 2026. We're committed to continued improvement in this area."

### 5.2 PREPARING FACULTY & STUDENTS FOR INTERVIEWS

#### Practice Interview Format

**Accreditor Interview Process:**

1. **Individual Interviews** (typically 30-45 minutes)
   - Accreditor meets with 1-2 faculty members or 1-2 students
   - Quiet, private room
   - Semi-structured interview (accreditor has questions, but flexible)
   - Conversation-like tone (not interrogation)

2. **Group Meetings** (30-60 minutes)
   - Accreditor meets with group (e.g., all program faculty, student cohort)
   - Discussion format
   - Accreditor asks questions; group participates

#### Practice Session

**Conduct Mock Interview (2-3 weeks before site visit):**

- [ ] Practice with small group of faculty
- [ ] Use sample accreditor questions (from Bagian 5.1)
- [ ] One person plays accreditor, others respond
- [ ] Practice professional demeanor & language
- [ ] Discuss how to handle difficult questions
- [ ] Provide feedback to each participant

**Script for Practice Interview:**

```
MODERATOR: "Let's do a practice accreditor interview. I'll ask questions 
similar to what you might get. Please answer as you would for the real visit."

ACCREDITOR (moderator): "Tell me about your program's learning outcomes. 
How do you know students are achieving them?"

FACULTY/STUDENT: [Gives answer]

MODERATOR: "Good answer. You provided specific outcome [X], gave assessment 
data [X], and showed improvement [X]. Let me ask a follow-up..."

[Continue with 4-5 more questions]

DEBRIEFING: "What went well? What could be stronger? How did that feel?"
```
