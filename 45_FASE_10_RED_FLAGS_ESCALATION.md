# FASE 10: RED FLAGS & ESCALATION PROCEDURES
## Critical Issues Response Protocol

**Applies to:** 1 Agustus - End of Semester  
**Owner:** Kaprodi + WK1  
**Purpose:** Rapid response to staffing/operational crises

---

## OVERVIEW & QUICK DECISION TREE

**When an issue occurs, ask:**

1. **Is someone's safety at risk?** → EMERGENCY (Call Dekan + security)
2. **Will classes be cancelled if not fixed TODAY?** → URGENT (Call WK1)
3. **Will this harm students if not fixed THIS WEEK?** → HIGH (Email Kaprodi)
4. **Is this a planning issue for next week?** → MEDIUM (Weekly report)
5. **Is this documentation for lessons learned?** → LOW (Archive for next year)

---

## TIER 1: EMERGENCY ESCALATION (SAME DAY - CALL NOW)

**Timeline:** Call within 1 hour of issue  
**Who to call:** Kaprodi → WK1 → Dekan (as needed)  
**Communication:** Phone call + follow-up email with documentation  
**Decision deadline:** Within 4 hours

### RED FLAG 1: DOSEN MULTIPLE NO-SHOWS (2+ in one week)

**Scenario:** 
- Dosen X didn't show up for Class A on Monday
- Same dosen missed Class B on Tuesday (no notice, no substitute)
- Class C scheduled for Wednesday is now at risk

**Impact:** 
- Students showing up to class with no lecturer
- Class cancellation affects academic calendar
- Student complaints & morale issues

**Immediate Action (Within 1 hour):**
```
☐ STEP 1: Contact dosen immediately
  Method: Call + WhatsApp message
  Message: "Your class was missed today. Status? Can you teach tomorrow?"
  Note: If unreachable after 15 min, escalate to STEP 2
  
☐ STEP 2: Call Kaprodi
  What to say: "Dosen [NAME] no-show Monday-Tuesday. Unreachable. 
  Wednesday class [MK CODE] at risk. Do we have substitute?"
  Get: Decision on Wednesday coverage
  
☐ STEP 3: Secure substitute for all affected classes
  Priority: Find dosen in SAME subject area
  Timeline: Must be confirmed by 3 PM same day
  Fallback: Reschedule class (inform students immediately)
  
☐ STEP 4: Notify students of changes
  Method: Email + WhatsApp group (if available)
  Message: "Class [CODE] on [DATE] will be taught by [SUBSTITUTE]" 
           OR "Class postponed to [NEW DATE]"
  Timeline: Before 5 PM today

☐ STEP 5: Document everything
  Record: Who was contacted, when, what was decided
  Save: All email/message evidence
  File: In incident log (File 46)
```

**Investigation & Follow-up (Next day):**
```
☐ Follow-up with dosen
  Question: Why no-show? Is there a medical/family emergency?
  Decision: Is dosen able to continue? Does dosen need support?
  
☐ Evaluate trend
  Question: Is this one-time incident or pattern?
  If pattern: Escalate to performance management
  
☐ Prevent recurrence
  Action: Implement daily check-in system? Email reminders? Mentoring?
  Timeline: Implement within 1 week
```

**Escalation Template:**
```
TO: Kaprodi
SUBJECT: URGENT - Dosen [NAME] Multiple No-Shows

Dear Kaprodi,

Dosen [NAME] missed classes on [DATES] without notice.

Classes affected:
- [MK CODE] on [DATE] (no-show)
- [MK CODE] on [DATE] (no-show)
- [MK CODE] on [DATE] (at risk)

Status:
✓ Dosen contacted: [TIME] - [RESPONSE]
✓ Substitute secured: [NAME] OR ☐ Seeking substitute
✓ Students notified: [TIME]
✓ Alternative: [Reschedule to DATE]

Requested decision: Can dosen teach Wednesday class, or should we use substitute?

Action taken: [LIST]

Regards,
[Name]
Academic Admin
```

### RED FLAG 2: LAB CLOSURE DUE TO STAFF SHORTAGE

**Scenario:**
- Lab technician called in sick
- Lab assistant just resigned
- Lab is scheduled to run Monday-Friday this week
- No one available to supervise practical sessions

**Impact:**
- All practical sessions (labs, workshops) cancelled
- Students can't complete required hands-on work
- Academic schedule disrupted
- Safety concerns if untrained person uses equipment

**Immediate Action (Within 2 hours):**
```
☐ STEP 1: Verify the situation
  Question: Is lab really closed? For how long?
  Contact: Lab manager + all available support staff
  Decision: Can we find ANY qualified person to supervise?
  
☐ STEP 2: Explore emergency solutions (in order of preference)
  Option A: Temporary contractor (same day hiring if available)
           Timeline: Call contractor agency NOW
           
  Option B: Reassign from another department (temporary swap)
           Timeline: Get manager agreement within 1 hour
           
  Option C: Senior student/TA supervision (if qualified)
           Timeline: Identify within 30 min
           
  Option D: Reschedule practical sessions
           Timeline: Find new dates by 5 PM today
           
  Option E: Online/theory alternative (temporary)
           Timeline: Design alternative by tomorrow

☐ STEP 3: Escalate to WK1 if no solution by 2 PM
  What to report: "Lab closed due to [REASON]. 
  Explored [OPTIONS]. Need institutional support for [SOLUTION]."
  
☐ STEP 4: Notify students & instructors immediately
  Message: "Lab session [DATE/TIME] is [CANCELLED / RESCHEDULED / USING ALTERNATIVE]
           New arrangements: [DETAIL]"
  Timeline: Before 5 PM same day
  
☐ STEP 5: Document for contingency planning
  Record: What happened, how we responded, outcome
  File: In contingency section (for future prevention)
```

**Long-term Follow-up:**
```
☐ Analyze staffing adequacy
  Question: Do we have backup staff? Cross-training?
  Action: Create job posting for replacement / hire temporary
  Timeline: Submit to HR within 3 days

☐ Prevent future closure
  Action: Build relief staff roster (for illness/emergencies)
  Timeline: Identify by end of week

☐ Support affected students
  Action: Offer make-up sessions or homework alternative
  Timeline: Schedule by end of week
```

### RED FLAG 3: SIAKNG SYSTEM DOWN (Affecting registration/core functions)

**Scenario:**
- Students can't login to SIAKNG
- Course registration menu is unavailable
- Grades can't be submitted
- Academic calendar affected

**Impact:**
- Course registration delayed (affects class assignments)
- Student anxiety about academic progress
- Faculty can't input grades (deadline issues)
- Cascading problems throughout semester

**Immediate Action (Within 30 minutes):**
```
☐ STEP 1: Verify the outage
  Action: Try login yourself
  Status: Can you access SIAKNG? YES / NO
  Contact: IT support immediately (phone, not email)
  
☐ STEP 2: Get ETA for resolution
  Question to IT: "When will SIAKNG be back online?"
  If <2 hours: Monitor & wait
  If >2 hours: Activate contingency plan (STEP 3)

☐ STEP 3: Activate offline contingency
  Option A: If registration deadline: Extend deadline email
           Message: "SIAKNG temporarily down. Registration deadline 
                    extended to [NEW DATE]"
           Timeline: Send within 1 hour
           
  Option B: If grade submission deadline: Give extension
           Message: "Grade submission deadline extended to [NEW DATE]
                    due to system outage"
           Timeline: Send within 1 hour
           
  Option C: If critical system: Switch to alternative (call IT)
           Ask: Is there a backup system? Manual process?

☐ STEP 4: Communicate with all stakeholders
  TO: Faculty
      "SIAKNG is down. Grade submissions are temporarily paused. 
       Expected resolution: [TIME]. New deadline: [DATE]"
  
  TO: Students
      "Course registration is temporarily paused due to system maintenance.
       New deadline: [DATE]. No penalty for late registration."
  
  TO: Kaprodi + WK1
      "SIAKNG down since [TIME]. IT working on resolution.
       ETA: [TIME]. Students/faculty communicated. 
       New deadline: [DATE] (extended by ___ days)"
  
  Timeline: All messages sent within 1 hour of outage

☐ STEP 5: Plan recovery
  After SIAKNG is back online:
  ├─ Test login (verify access works)
  ├─ Confirm all data intact (no loss)
  ├─ Send "System back online" message to all users
  ├─ Provide new deadline for registrations/submissions
  └─ Plan follow-up with IT on prevention
```

### RED FLAG 4: ABK STAFF RESIGNATION (Without notice or critical role)

**Scenario:**
- Academic admin staff member submits resignation (effective immediately)
- OR support staff just quit during semester
- Critical functions now at risk (course registration, student records)

**Impact:**
- Institutional knowledge walks out the door
- Critical processes delayed
- Workload overload for remaining staff
- Student service quality drops

**Immediate Action (Within 1 hour):**
```
☐ STEP 1: Verify & document the resignation
  Confirm: Is this official? Get written resignation letter
  Timeline: Within 30 min
  
☐ STEP 2: Identify critical functions at risk
  Question: What was this person's key responsibilities?
  Map: Which processes/students affected?
  Priority: Rank functions by urgency (A = today, B = this week, C = later)
  
☐ STEP 3: Triage critical work
  For each critical function:
  ├─ WHO can immediately take over (next senior staff member)?
  ├─ WHAT support do they need (training, time, resources)?
  ├─ WHEN do they need to start (today)?
  └─ HOW to backfill other duties?
  
  Decision: Assign immediate owner for each critical task

☐ STEP 4: Notify affected parties
  TO: Kaprodi + WK1
      "Staff [NAME] resigned effective [DATE]. 
       Critical functions at risk: [LIST].
       Immediate measures: [ACTION PLAN].
       HR support needed for: [REPLACEMENT TIMELINE]"
  
  TO: Relevant faculty
      "Course registration processing will have [DELAY/ALTERNATE PROCESS]
       New timeline: [DATE]"
  
  Timeline: Within 2 hours

☐ STEP 5: Request HR support
  Ask: Can we hire replacement immediately (contract/temporary)?
  Timeline: Submit request TODAY
  Justification: Critical function cannot wait

☐ STEP 6: Stabilize & prevent burnout
  Action: Redistribute workload (don't overload remaining staff)
  Action: Hire temporary/contract staff within 1 week
  Action: Check in with remaining staff (morale, support)
```

**Follow-up Plan:**
```
☐ Within 1 week: Get replacement hired
☐ Within 2 weeks: New staff trained & up to speed
☐ Debrief: What caused resignation? How to prevent next time?
☐ Update: Staff retention plan / workload assessment
```

### RED FLAG 5: SAFETY INCIDENT IN LAB/CAMPUS

**Scenario:**
- Equipment malfunction causes injury
- Safety violation (improper use of equipment, etc.)
- Chemical spill or electrical hazard
- Student/staff injury in lab

**Impact:**
- Immediate risk to students & staff
- Legal liability
- Lab closure pending investigation
- Reputational risk

**Immediate Action (Within 15 minutes):**
```
☐ STEP 1: Ensure immediate safety
  IF injury: Call ambulance/first aid
  IF hazard: Evacuate area, secure equipment
  Timeline: DO THIS FIRST (before anything else)

☐ STEP 2: Notify management chain
  Call in order:
  1. Lab manager
  2. Facilities/safety officer
  3. Kaprodi
  4. WK1
  5. Dekan (if serious)
  
  Message: "Safety incident in [LAB]. [BRIEF DESCRIPTION]. 
            Action taken: [IMMEDIATE RESPONSE]. 
            Status: [ONGOING / CONTROLLED]"

☐ STEP 3: Document incident
  Record: What happened, when, who was involved, what was done
  Photo: (if safe) of hazard/incident
  Witness: Names of people present
  Evidence: Preserve equipment/materials for investigation

☐ STEP 4: Secure the area
  Action: Post warning signs, lock down equipment
  Goal: Prevent others from accessing hazard

☐ STEP 5: Plan investigation
  Owner: Safety officer + Kaprodi
  Timeline: Investigate within 1 week
  Outcome: Report to WK1 + Dekan + insurance (if injury)

☐ STEP 6: Plan reopening
  Question: Can lab reopen tomorrow, or does investigation take longer?
  Alternative: Reschedule practical sessions if lab closed >1 day
  Timeline: Decide by end of day
```

**Investigation & Prevention:**
```
Within 1 week:
☐ Completed investigation report
☐ Root cause identified
☐ Preventive actions documented
☐ Training/protocol updates needed

Within 2 weeks:
☐ All staff trained on new safety procedures
☐ Equipment repair/replacement completed
☐ Lab cleared for reopening
☐ Report submitted to administration
```

### RED FLAG 6: Dosen frequent sick days (>2x per month)

**Scenario:**
- Dosen X has been sick 3x in past 4 weeks
- Pattern emerging (always Monday/Friday? certain time of month?)
- Other dosen covering, but becoming unsustainable

**Assessment:**
```
☐ Is this a legitimate health issue?
  Option A: Serious illness (recover soon) → Arrange extended leave
  Option B: Chronic illness → Permanent accommodation needed
  Option C: Suspected abuse → Require medical documentation
  
☐ Impact on classes?
  Question: Are all classes covered? Students satisfied?
  If covered well: Monitor, but no urgent action needed
  If struggling: Need staffing plan

☐ Conversation with dosen
  Goal: Understand situation, offer support
  Approach: "We noticed you've been unwell frequently. 
            How can we support your health/work situation?"
  Listen: Is this a personal crisis? Health issue? Workload problem?
  Plan: Work together on solution
```

**Actions:**
```
☐ Document all absences & reasons (keep confidential record)
☐ Ensure classes are covered (assign substitute if needed)
☐ Offer support (flexible schedule, workload adjustment, etc.)
☐ Monitor trend over next 4 weeks
☐ If pattern continues: Move to performance management
☐ Report to HR if accommodation needed (medical leave, ADA, etc.)
```

### RED FLAG 7: Student complaints about teaching quality

**Scenario:**
- Multiple students complain about a dosen's teaching
- Feedback: Unprepared, unfair grading, disorganized
- Pattern or one-time complaint?

**Assessment:**
```
☐ Gather detailed feedback
  How many complaints? ___
  From which classes? ___
  Specific issues: [LIST]
  
☐ Verify legitimacy
  Are complaints from credible sources?
  Is feedback specific or vague?
  Is this sour grapes or legitimate concern?

☐ Consult other sources
  Other instructors' experience with dosen?
  Previous years' feedback?
  Dosen's track record?
```

**Actions:**
```
☐ If serious concerns:
  Step 1: Kaprodi meets with dosen (1-on-1 conversation)
  Step 2: Discuss concerns, ask for dosen's perspective
  Step 3: Offer support (mentoring, teaching training, etc.)
  Step 4: Agree on improvement plan with timeline
  
☐ If minor feedback:
  Step 1: Informally share feedback with dosen
  Step 2: Offer development resources
  Step 3: Monitor improvement over next 4 weeks

☐ Document & follow up
  Record: Feedback received, action taken, outcome
  Timeline: Check-in after 4 weeks
  Decision: Improvement satisfactory, or escalate to HR?
```

### RED FLAG 8: Document processing backlog >1 week

**Scenario:**
- Student transcripts taking 10 days to process (target: 3 days)
- Course registration forms not processed in time
- Grade records backlogged

**Impact:**
- Student frustration & complaints
- Academic delays
- Staff burnout

**Assessment:**
```
☐ Root cause analysis
  Why the backlog? 
  - Understaffing? 
  - Process inefficiency? 
  - Unexpected high volume?
  - System issues?

☐ Measure the impact
  How many students affected? ___
  What's the delay? ___
  
☐ Feasibility of quick fix
  Can we speed up by this week?
  Or will it take longer?
```

**Actions:**
```
☐ Immediate (this week):
  ├─ Identify bottleneck in process
  ├─ Reassign staff to clear backlog (if possible)
  ├─ Extend deadlines for affected students
  ├─ Inform students of new timeline
  └─ Status: Expect resolution by [DATE]

☐ Short-term (next 2 weeks):
  ├─ Implement process improvement (faster procedure?)
  ├─ Hire temporary staff if needed
  ├─ Increase automation (if possible)
  └─ Monitor: Ensure backlog doesn't recur

☐ Long-term planning:
  ├─ Is permanent staffing increase needed?
  ├─ Are processes fundamentally broken?
  ├─ What's sustainable for future?
```

### RED FLAG 9: Lab equipment broken, no timeline to fix

**Scenario:**
- Key lab equipment (computer, instrument, etc.) broke down
- Repair technician says "it will take a few weeks"
- Practical sessions depending on this equipment are at risk

**Assessment:**
```
☐ Criticality: How important is this equipment?
  Essential (no workaround): URGENT
  Important (can do alternatives): MODERATE
  Nice-to-have (can defer): LOW

☐ Timeline impact: When is it needed?
  Practical session next week: URGENT
  Practical session in 3 weeks: MODERATE
  Not needed this semester: LOW

☐ Repair feasibility:
  Can we get it fixed faster? (rush fee?)
  Can we borrow equipment temporarily?
  Can we rent equipment short-term?
  Can we adjust curriculum (use theory instead)?
```

**Actions:**
```
☐ Immediate:
  ├─ Contact equipment manufacturer for RUSH repair
  ├─ Get definitive repair timeline (ask for written estimate)
  ├─ Explore alternatives (borrow, rent, substitute equipment)
  └─ Inform instructors of timeline

☐ If repair >1 week:
  ├─ Adjust curriculum for affected classes
  ├─ Option A: Use alternative equipment/methods
  ├─ Option B: Rent replacement equipment
  ├─ Option C: Reschedule practical to after repair
  ├─ Get student approval for alternative (fair? meets learning goals?)
  └─ Document accommodation in course records

☐ Report to Kaprodi:
  "Equipment [NAME] broken. Repair timeline: [WEEKS].
   Impact: Practical session [DATE] at risk.
   Mitigation: [ALTERNATIVE].
   Approval needed: [YES/NO]"

☐ Follow-up:
  Once fixed: Verify equipment works
  Quality check: Is repair adequate?
  Prevention: Can we prevent future breakdowns? (maintenance plan)
```

### RED FLAG 10: ABK staff requesting leave (during critical period)

**Scenario:**
- Admin staff wants to take vacation leave during course registration period
- Support staff requests medical leave
- Multiple staff requesting leave at same time

**Assessment:**
```
☐ When is the leave requested?
  During critical period? (registration, exams, etc.)
  Or calm period?
  
☐ Is coverage available?
  Can other staff cover these duties?
  Or will critical functions be disrupted?

☐ Is the request legitimate?
  Medical need?
  Pre-approved annual leave?
  Emergency (family situation)?
```

**Actions:**
```
☐ If during critical period:
  Step 1: Politely discuss with staff member
          "We have heavy workload [PERIOD]. Can you reschedule?"
  
  Step 2: Explore compromises
          ├─ Partial leave (part of the period)?
          ├─ Work some hours remotely?
          ├─ Hire temporary to cover?
          └─ Redistribute to other staff?
  
  Step 3: If still needed (medical/emergency):
          ├─ Approve leave
          ├─ Hire temporary ASAP
          ├─ Cross-train coverage
          └─ Adjust deadlines for affected functions

☐ If routine period:
  Approve without issue
  Plan: Cross-train for coverage
  Document: Leave calendar for future planning

☐ Prevent future issues:
  Create: Leave calendar (known heavy periods flagged)
  Policy: Advanced notice for vacation requests
  Staffing: Extra coverage during critical periods
```
